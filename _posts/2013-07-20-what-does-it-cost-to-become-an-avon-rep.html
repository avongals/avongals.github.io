--- layout: blog_signup title: "What does it cost to become an Avon rep?" author: paul tags: - Avon - representative ---

When you become an Avon representative, there is a small joining fee of £15. This applies to everybody and covers your start up costs with Avon, including:


  • Startup Avon brochures (usually for your first few campaigns)
  • Admin costs
  • Startup literature

The good thing is that this is the only fee you pay, and it is spread over your 1st and 2nd campaigns. Spreading the cost like this means that you generally make some profit when you first start!


Here's an example:

First campaign:

£200 - Total Orders
- £150 - Cost of products (with 25% discount)
- £7.50 - First admin fee
= £42.50 - Profit

Second campaign:

£200 - Total Orders
- £150 - Cost of products (with 25% discount)
- £7.50 - Second admin fee
= £42.50 - Profit


What happens if you decide Avon isn't for you?

If you quit before your first campaign - you won't pay any admin fee.
If you quit before your second campaign - you will only pay half the admin fee


How does this compare to other direct selling companies?

Ann Summers - £112
Jamie at Home - £100
Kleeneze - £75 / £135

As you can see, Avon requires much less investment!

The startup costs are lower than other Direct Selling companies because you buy products on credit, rather than buying products out of your own money. Since the £15 admin fee comes out of your profits, most people don't even notice it.
